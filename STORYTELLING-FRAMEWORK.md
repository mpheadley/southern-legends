# Southern Legends — Strategy & Storytelling Framework

*A reference document for Claude Code project sessions*

---

## What Southern Legends Is

Southern Legends is a community business profile project based in Northeast Alabama. It serves three purposes simultaneously:

1. **Community building** — Telling the stories of local businesses, makers, and creatives in the Calhoun County area and beyond.
2. **Lead generation for Headley Web & SEO** — Free profiles serve as a value-first hook that opens natural web design conversations.
3. **Personal healing** — Profiling people rebuilds relationships Matt thought he'd lost because of decisions made while manic. The work of sitting across from someone, listening carefully, and telling their story honestly is part of how he's finding his way back. This isn't the pitch — it's the private truth underneath the project.

Profiles are approximately 1,000 words. The goal is not a business directory listing — it's a story that makes the reader feel something.

## The Storytelling Frameworks Behind It

### StoryBrand
- The business owner is the **hero** of their own profile.
- Matt (as the profile writer) is the **guide** helping them tell that story well.
- The **reader** is also invited into the story — they recognize themselves in the owner's journey or values.
- The profile draws people in. It's not a hard sell. It's relational, not transactional.

### The Lowry Loop (Narrative Preaching Method)
Matt's background in ministry and preaching connects directly to how Southern Legends profiles are structured:

- **Observation** — Here's who this person is and what they care about.
- **Complication** — Here's the challenge they face, the gap they fill, the stakes involved.
- **Resolution** — Here's how they show up for their community.

The profile *preaches*, in the best sense — it invites the reader to recognize something true about themselves through someone else's story.

### The Cross-Pollination
Both frameworks share the same DNA: create tension, then release it. Both invite the listener/reader to see *themselves* in the story being told. Donald Miller's insight applies here — the best story you can tell isn't really about you. It's about the person listening, who sees themselves in it.

## The Funnel

**Southern Legends** (public) → draws readers in, builds community awareness, introduces profiles to anyone who finds them.

**Patreon** (inner circle) → deepens the relationship for readers ready to support the work financially. Supporters get access to longer reflections, deeper storytelling, and Matt's ongoing journey.

**Headley Web & SEO** (conversion) → the natural next conversation for business owners who've been profiled and want the same quality of care applied to their web presence.

## Matt's Own Profile

Matt intends to publish his own Southern Legends profile. Key distinction:

- **headleyweb.com bio** — Professional, business-focused. For clients evaluating whether to hire him.
- **Southern Legends profile** — More personal and community-oriented. Can include the real story of loss, rebuilding, and what brought him to this work. Appropriate for vulnerability that doesn't belong on a services page.
- **Patreon deep dive** — The full arc. The psychiatric hospitalization at UAB, learning to play dominoes with a friend named Jack in the ward, losing his car, moving back in with his parents, the disorientation — and the slow rebuilding into meaningful work. This is for people who want to support the whole person and the whole journey.

### Emotional Texture / Tone Reference
The song *Crazy* (Cee Lo Green) resonates with the emotional texture of that season — not for its literal narrative, but for the feeling it evokes: disorientation, questioning yourself, not having solid ground. That emotional register is relevant to the Patreon content and possibly to the Southern Legends profile.

The phrase **"Broken and becoming"** captures the arc of Matt's personal story and should inform the tone of his profile.

## The Patreon

### What It Is
A rebooted version of a Patreon Matt launched before his bipolar diagnosis and hospitalization. People who followed it through that period may witness the transformation of both the work and the person — that's part of what makes rebooting it meaningful rather than just starting fresh.

### What Transformation It Offers Supporters
- From disconnection → belonging (knowing and caring about your community's stories)
- From passive → active (becoming someone who sustains cultural storytelling)
- For some: supporting a person they believe in who is rebuilding with intention

### What It Is Not
It's not primarily a StoryBrand conversion tool — it's a support and community platform. The transformation isn't the patron's alone. They're investing in watching someone rebuild and create meaningful work.

### Format
Longer-form reflective writing — blog-style, not profile-style. Space for Matt to explore things that don't fit in a 1,000-word profile: the psychiatric ward, the people he met there, the mushroom trip, what he learned, what he's still processing. Patreon is the inner circle; Southern Legends is the public face.

## Tone & Voice Notes

- StoryBrand is the conversion framework. Lowry loop is the narrative soul.
- Profiles should feel like they were written by someone who understands both craft and community.
- Matt's ministry background isn't incidental — it's the reason he knows how to invite people into a story rather than just report on a business.
- Southern Legends is not a marketing product. It's a storytelling project that also happens to generate leads.
