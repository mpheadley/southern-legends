Blacksmithing
Earl McKinney: The Last Coal Forge on Highway 9
Piedmont, AL · January 15, 2026 · 8 min read

The shop smells like iron and burning coal. It's a smell Earl McKinney has known since he was twelve years old, standing at his grandfather's anvil, learning to read the color of heated steel.

"People ask me why I don't switch to gas. I tell 'em — you can't hear the metal talk over a gas forge."
Forty Years at the Anvil

He started full-time in 1986, the year his grandfather's hands finally gave out. The shop was paid for. The tools were waiting. All he had to do was show up and keep the fire lit.

"My granddaddy said the anvil don't care what kind of day you're having. You show up and hit it right, or you don't."
