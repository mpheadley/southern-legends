## Key Themes & Surprising Elements

### 1. Postpartum Anxiety Crisis
- Diagnosed with **postpartum anxiety** (not depression) after Kingston's birth
- Manifested as hypervigilance: "no one else can take care of this baby but me"
- Couldn't work, couldn't walk, couldn't leave the house
- Friend (Lisa Gillespie) came over, laid on her back, talked her through panic attacks
- Led to repossession of cars and financial collapse
- **Important:** She's proactive about medication now (took meds 6 hours after Maverick's birth)

### 2. JSU Teaching Years — Complicated Legacy
- Dream job: teaching ensemble at Jacksonville State University
- Pandemic timing, Dr. Baptist's mentorship that wasn't
- Tension with white students (Pam, Stephanie, Haley) — Jean holds ongoing bitterness
  - **CONSTRAINT:** These are friends of Matt & Heather's. Names must be anonymized in the piece.
- Dr. White explicitly told her she wasn't "physically" right for certain roles
- Contract not renewed over disputed reasons
- **Pattern Matt noticed:** Jean dwells on these grievances, retells the stories repeatedly

### 3. Pivots to Entrepreneurship
- **Music Box:** Born from postpartum recovery + library media degree (2013)
  - Went from needing income → building a business that let her be present for kids
  - Now has two locations on Noble Street, growing exponentially
  - Wants franchises; sees it as legacy business
  
- **Mom-To-Go:** Shifted from catering to teaching cooking classes
  - "Mom to go is who I am"
  - Wants Martha Stewart/Ina Garten level brand, products at Walmart/Target
  - Hot pink aesthetic (very intentional branding)
  - Wants to be a millionaire

### 4. The Cost of Building When You're "Not Supposed to Succeed"
- Jean was told (explicitly and implicitly) she wasn't the right type:
  - Wrong look (Black, bigger body)
  - Wrong credentials (teaching job ended)
  - Too sick to work (postpartum anxiety)
  - Too "difficult" (JSU narrative)
- Most people fold. Jean built anyway.
- **The defiance + healing are two sides of the same coin.** She healed *by building*.

## Structural Ideas

### Opening
- **Not with Mom-To-Go intro.** Maybe:
  - A moment from the call itself (her laugh about Matt being late for class)
  - OR: A specific scene from her life now (teaching a cooking class, running the Music Box)
  - Let reader *meet* Jean in real time before going backward

### Arc
1. **Who Jean is now** (present-day scene or interview moment)
2. **The college/teaching years** (JSU, Weaver, the pattern of being told "no") — *without naming Pam, Stephanie, Haley*
3. **The postpartum crisis** (the specific, devastating details: panic attacks, the friend on her back, repossession)
4. **The pivot** (Music Box born from ash; Mom-To-Go as next evolution)
5. **Who she's becoming** (the dream, the legacy, the defiance)

### Tone
- Celebrate her AND hold the complexity
- Don't validate the bitterness uncritically, but show it with compassion
- Matt's own weariness with the retelling could be present (subtly)
- "Let people be complicated" — Jean experienced real hurt at JSU, AND she also dwells on it in ways that feel stuck
