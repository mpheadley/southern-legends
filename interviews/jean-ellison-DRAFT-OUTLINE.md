# Jean Ellison Profile — Draft Outline

## I. OPENING: The Reunion

**Scene/moment:** The late-for-class callback
- Matt jokes about being late to JSU class
- Jean remembers and calls him out
- "Am I late for practice? What is it? No. No, no, no. We'd be calling you Matt."
- Establishes: Matt and Jean's history, the warmth between them, the weight of shared time

**Transition to:** But you don't know what happened between then and now.

## II. THE POSTPARTUM ANXIETY CRISIS

**Subsection A: The Perfect Plan (Collapses)**
- Jean planned everything right: college → pastor → baby → job
- Pregnant with Kingston, got job at Weaver High School starting August
- "When you plan, God laughs"
- Shift: Everything fell apart

**Subsection B: The Hypervigilance**
- Postpartum anxiety (not depression) — the "no one else can care for this baby" kind
- Couldn't work, couldn't walk, couldn't leave the house
- The specific paranoia: germs, hands, giving him a "brain-eating"
- Nursing all day/night; Steve saying "ain't no baby in this world that hungry"

**Subsection C: The Dissociation Moment** *(if Jean approves detail level)*
- Exhaustion from 5 AM wake-ups to teach full day
- In the classroom: walls closing in
- "I thought I was on a boat"
- Had to go to the hospital
- Doctor: "Have you heard of sleep exhaustion?"
- Jean's response: "I'm type A. I don't get exhausted."

**Subsection D: The Concrete Help (Lisa Gillespie)**
- Didn't want anyone to see her, locked in house
- Best friend Lisa came over
- Jean panicked: "don't get near me"
- Lisa laid on her back: "Lay on your back. Lay on your back."
- Lisa was a newly licensed therapist; Jean was one of her first cases
- Lisa talked her through panic attacks
- This is where Jean started to heal

**Subsection E: The Cost**
- Couldn't work = no income
- Repossession of cars (brand new Altima, had two masters degrees)
- Financial collapse alongside the mental health crisis
- "I never, ever, ever judge"

**Subsection F: The Medication & Honesty**
- "I take my medicine"
- Current practices: sunshine, movement, focus
- "We're doing the best we can, Matt"

## III. THE MUSIC BOX: SURVIVAL BECOMES BUILDING

**Subsection A: No Income, No Options**
- During recovery, she had no income
- Started feeling stronger
- Lisa: "Gene, your legs do work"

**Subsection B: The Unexpected Degree**
- Master's in library media (online, small cohort)
- Learned about slideshows, media, creative production
- Dropped out of final internship: "I was like, no"
- But the degree gave her the tools

**Subsection C: The Napkin Moment**
- "I could just teach music. I don't have to be in a classroom."
- May, Maverick was 10 months old
- Wrote a 10-page business plan
- Gave it to Steve; he looked at her like "okay"

**Subsection D: The Launch & Growth**
- Music Box born from postpartum anxiety, loss, and defiance
- "That's how the Music Box started, was through my postpartum anxiety"
- Two locations on Noble Street now
- Exponential growth
- But it wasn't smooth: December eviction (building sold)
- "Is this how the Music Box ends? No."
- Found new location, better than before

**Key theme:** She healed by building. Survival became business.

## IV. THE JSU YEARS: WHERE THE HURT LIVES

**Subsection A: The Dream Job**
- Teaching ensemble at Jacksonville State University
- Hired 3 months before the pandemic
- Had to teach virtually, then masked and distanced
- Changed the culture: jerseys and Converse (everyone loved it)
- Did Hamilton, challenging pieces
- ~4 years of teaching there
- Students cried when she left
- "It was a dream job. It's just wrong timing."

**Subsection B: The Mentor Who Wasn't (Dr. Baptiste)**
- Dr. Baptiste was supposed to be her mentor (since Jean was a junior in college)
- Pandemic complications: virtual teaching, complaints about difficulty level
- Some students said Jean was "sarcastic," "tough," "hard"
- But majority of students: "They cried when I left"
- Dr. Baptiste didn't renew her contract
- Official excuse: "Position no longer available"
- Reality: Jean was making $500/month for 4 days/week + scholarships, shows, intern supervision
- Dr. Baptiste only complemented Jean's appearance (dreads, outfits), never mentored

**Subsection C: The Institutional Rejection (Dr. White)** *(exact context varies depending on what Jean approves)*
- Dr. White explicitly told Jean she wasn't "physically" right for certain roles
- "You're just not my choice physically or visually for this"
- But also: "nobody could sing it though, like you, so there's that"
- Jean came home and sobbed
- White students whispered during her solo
- The contradiction: she had the talent, but not the "look"

**Subsection D: The Dwelling**
- Jean carries this. She retells it. It shaped her.
- "I would like to send him a letter about the abuse over the years"
- The pattern: told she wasn't right for the role, the look, the place
- But she also realizes now: "I'm not going to minimize myself"
- At 41, choosing herself

**Key tension:** Jean experienced real institutional hurt (racism + appearance policing + mentor betrayal). She dwells on it. Both things are true.

## V. MOM-TO-GO: THE VISION

**Subsection A: The Identity**
- "Mom to Go is who I am"
- "Mom to Go is the mom on the go"
- Boys are her life; entrepreneurship lets her be present
- Shifted from catering to teaching cooking classes
- "Everyone who knows me, you know I love to feed you"

**Subsection B: The Martha Stewart Dream**
- Martha Stewart, Ina Garten level
- Walmart, Target, nationwide brand
- Hot pink everything (intentional branding)
- Recipe book as the stepping stone
- Aprons, tableware, cutlery — full lifestyle brand

**Subsection C: The Defiance**
- "I want to be a millionaire, Matt"
- Not apologetic, not explaining, just claiming it
- After being told she wasn't "right" at JSU
- After losing cars and income to postpartum crisis
- Building anyway

---

## VI. CLOSING: THE CHOICE

**Possible directions:**
- Circle back to the resilience question (why does she keep building?)
- Return to the JSU hurt, but show how she's moved beyond letting it define her
- End with the millionaire ambition — what that means to her
- Or: the defiance + complexity together (she's ambitious AND she dwells on JSU; both are true)

**Avoid:** Wrapping it up neatly, making her a symbol of resilience, inspirational arc

**Aim for:** Complexity held, Jean as a full person — grievance + vision, hurt + ambition

## NOTES

- **Opening:** Late-for-class warmth, then pivot to the crisis
- **Emotional arc:** Reunion → Crisis → Survival → Building → Looking back at what hurt her → Claiming her future

- **Tone:** Literary journalism, not brochure. Warm but unsentimental. Notice what Jean did without calling it inspiring.
- **The dwelling is real:** Jean retells the JSU hurt. Don't sanitize that. But also: she's aware of it now, trying to move past it.
