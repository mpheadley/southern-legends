Texture — Subtle paper grain on warm sections?

A very light noise overlay on warm sections to reinforce the "Parchment" feel. Compare flat vs. textured.

Flat (current)

Clean and minimal. Lets the type do all the work.

With paper grain

Adds subtle warmth and tactility. Reinforces the editorial/analog vibe.

Recommendation

Paper grain is a small touch that reinforces the "Parchment & Pine" identity — especially when no photos are pulling visual weight. It's CSS-only, no image download, and can be applied selectively (warm sections, card image areas, detail hero backgrounds). Easy to remove later if it feels heavy.

Decision 5 of 5

AI Image Generation — Where (if anywhere)?

Should any AI-generated imagery be used, and if so, where?

Hero portraits of profiled people

No. These are real people (or will be). An AI-generated face would be dishonest and uncanny. The whole point of the site is authenticity — a fake portrait undermines that immediately.

Atmospheric / scene illustrations

Maybe. An AI-generated illustration of a forge, a kitchen, a workshop — used as a decorative element (not claiming to be the person's actual space) — could work. But it adds a layer of artificiality to a project about real places.

Textures & patterns

Yes. Paper grain, linen weave, subtle noise — AI or procedural generation is perfect here. No one expects these to be "real." They're design elements, not content.

OG / social share images

Maybe. A branded typographic OG image (name + category + colors) is more honest and distinctive than a generic AI scene. But an AI-generated background texture behind the typography could enhance it.

Recommendation

Typography only (no AI images) is the strongest position for this project. It's authentic, distinctive, and aligns with the editorial tone. Use CSS-generated textures for the parchment feel. When real photography arrives (Phase 6), the typographic system becomes a graceful fallback — cards with photos show photos, cards without show the initial/name treatment. No awkward "some AI, some real" mismatch.
