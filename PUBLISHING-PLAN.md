## Publishing Sequence

For each piece of content, follow this order:

1. **Southern Legends first** — Publish on your own site. You own the content, your direct audience sees it, and it establishes the original publication.
2. **Anniston Star second** — Pitch to Donna Barton after 1–2 weeks. The Star has a paid model, so they get the larger-outlet exclusivity window after your site.
3. **Calhoun Journal third** — Syndicate after another 1–2 weeks. Their free model means prior publication elsewhere isn't a problem, and the "as seen in the Anniston Star" credential strengthens the pitch.

---

## Why This Order Works

- You own the original publication and control the narrative.
- The Star's paid audience gets content before the free Calhoun Journal audience, respecting their business model.
- Each outlet gets something that feels fresh to their readers.
- By the time it reaches the Calhoun Journal, you have publication history in two places, which builds credibility.
- The Star's audience is significantly larger than Southern Legends' current reach, so prior publication on your site doesn't reduce their value proposition.

## Format Decision

Written essays for now, not video. Written form is more intimate and lets readers sit with the material. Video profiles make sense for Southern Legends interviews (spotlighting others), but the personal journal work is stronger as written first. You can repurpose into video later if it lands well.

---

## Byline Strategy

**Recommended byline for both the Anniston Star and the Calhoun Journal:**

> Matt Headley is a former pastor, classically trained singer, and flower farmer. He writes local business profiles and personal essays about mental health and faith at Southern Legends (southernlegends.blog). He runs Headley Web & SEO in Jacksonville, Alabama.

**Key points:**

- Only one link: Southern Legends. Keeps the byline clean and editorial rather than promotional.
- Headley Web & SEO mentioned in plain text — still provides brand recognition and local SEO benefit without a second link.
- Southern Legends mentioned before Headley Web & SEO since that's the content readers are engaging with.
- The pastor / singer / flower farmer framing establishes you as a human with a story, not just a service provider.
- Description of the Southern Legends content sets expectations for both business profiles and personal essays.

**Send the exact byline to editors when pitching** — editors sometimes rewrite bylines, so being upfront prevents surprises.

## SEO & Business Benefits

- Backlinks from the Anniston Star and Calhoun Journal to Southern Legends boost domain authority.
- Headley Web & SEO gets brand recognition in reputable publications even without a direct link.
- People searching your name find your work across multiple established outlets.
- Editorial trust converts to warmer cold outreach — prospects like Lewis at Downing and Sons encounter your story before your pitch.
- Google rewards fresh content and authoritative backlinks, which compounds over time.
