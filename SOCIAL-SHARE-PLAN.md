## Journal Launch Sequence

### Step 1 — Personal DMs (before any public post)
Send to 3–5 people this piece would hit specifically — people who knew you during the silence, who know what the farm loss cost. Not a list. Just the ones who come to mind.

Already done: personal messages to 4 Patreon supporters (April 14, 2026).

---

### Step 2 — First public share: "I'm Not Going to Disappear"
**When:** Today (April 15) or tomorrow morning.

**Why this one first:** It's the reintroduction. Names where you've been (diagnosis, hospital, hiding) and ends with you showing up again. Reads like a letter to your community without being called one. Leads with the most current, alive energy.

**Personal Facebook post — write in your own voice, not the excerpt:**
> I published something today. It's about where I've been.
> [link]

That's enough. Don't oversell it.

**Then:** Share the post from the Southern Legends Facebook page. Tag your personal profile.

---

### Step 3 — Second share: "The Same Domain"
**When:** 2–3 days after Step 2.

**Why:** The meta-piece. Works better as the second thing people read — once they know the journal exists. Explains why personal writing lives on the same site as the business profiles. Good for people who know you're building Headley Web.

**Personal Facebook post:**
> I published something about why a personal journal and a business website are on the same domain.
> [link]

### Step 4 — Hold for now

- **Attempt 73** — same. Better as the 3rd or 4th share.
- **Hope in the Wilderness** — already ran in the Anniston Star. Most local people have seen it.
- **My Writing Has a New Home** — Patreon closing post. Doesn't stand alone well for a cold audience.

---

## Rules
- One piece per share session. Don't post two in the same day.
- Personal post first, SL page share after.
- Write the Facebook post in your own voice — never use the excerpt verbatim.
- Don't explain too much. Short post, let the piece do the work.
- Space posts at least 2–3 days apart.
