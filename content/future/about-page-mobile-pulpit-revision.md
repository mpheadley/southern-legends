# Future Content: About Page Revision — The Mobile Pulpit Thread

**Format:** Revision to the existing About page (`src/app/about/page.tsx`)
**Target section:** "Who's Behind This?"
**What changes:** Weave the "mobile pulpit" idea into the editorial voice framing — not as a Patreon callback, but as the quiet thesis of the site.

---

## The Idea

The current About page bio is functional but flat: "I'm a web designer. I started this because I kept meeting people with good stories." It doesn't have a point of view about *why Matt specifically* is the right person to tell these stories.

The mobile pulpit concept fills that gap — without using the phrase itself (which is Patreon-specific and too churchy for this context). The underlying idea: Matt spent twenty years in a vocation built around listening to people, honoring their stories, and creating spaces where community happens. That vocation changed form, but the impulse didn't. Southern Legends is pastoral work in a different register.

## Revised "Who's Behind This?" — Direction

Keep the headshot, keep the Headley Web link. But replace the two paragraphs after the bio blurb with something that connects the editorial instinct to the pastoral background — without making it a confessional or a résumé.

**Beats to hit:**

1. **The skill underneath the skill:** "I spent a long time in work that was really about one thing: sitting with people and paying attention to what mattered to them. The context changed — I'm not behind a pulpit anymore — but the instinct didn't."

2. **Why that matters for this project:** The profiles aren't journalism (no pretense of objectivity). They're not marketing (no client relationship). They're closer to what happens when someone who's spent years listening to people decides to write down what he hears. That's the editorial voice of the site.

3. **Light touch on the transition:** One sentence, no drama. "I came to web design and storytelling after a career change that wasn't entirely voluntary — you can read about that [in my profile]." Let the profile carry the weight.

## What NOT to do

- Don't use the phrase "mobile pulpit" — it's too insider, too Patreon-specific
- Don't turn the About page into a second profile — it should be about the site, not about Matt
- Don't add theological language — "pastoral" is fine as an adjective describing an approach, not as a religious identity
- Don't make it longer than it currently is — tighter is better. Replace, don't expand.
