# Coldwater Mountain — Research Notes

## Facts
- **Full name:** Coldwater Mountain
- **Location:** Between Anniston and Oxford, Alabama (Calhoun County)
- **Category:** Places
- **Land type:** 4,083-acre Alabama Forever Wild tract
- **Trail miles:** Over 56 miles of singletrack (professionally designed)
- **IMBA status:** Bronze Ride Center, pursuing Silver
- **Investment:** $4 million+ in trail development since 2012
- **Master plan:** 2012, IMBA Trail Solutions, designed for 60 miles
- **Key organization:** Northeast Alabama Bicycle Association (NEABA) — volunteer trail maintenance, advocacy
- **Notable person:** Tom Nelson, former NEABA president
- **Innovate Alabama investment:** $1.1 million in trail improvements
- **Leadville qualifier:** October 2025, Life Time chose Coldwater for first Leadville Trail 100 qualifying event east of the Mississippi — 600+ athletes from 34 states and Canada
- **Life Time scouting:** 4 years scouting Southeast locations before picking Anniston
- **Key trails:** Bomb Dog (signature, flow/views), Gazza (rocky/technical), Southern Traverse (8.5 mi intermediate connector, newest)
- **Trailheads:** Coldwater Trailhead (1661 Coldwater Road), Anniston Trailhead (401 Monsanto Street)
- **Hours:** Sunrise to 1-2 hours after sunset, free parking
- **State designation:** 2025 = "Year of Alabama Trails," Coldwater was centerpiece
- **Trail info:** Trailforks, Coldwater Mountain Facebook page
