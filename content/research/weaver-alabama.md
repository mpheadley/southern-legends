# Weaver, Alabama — Research Notes

## Facts
- **Name:** Weaver, Alabama
- **Location:** Calhoun County, between Anniston and Jacksonville
- **Category:** Places (Community / Small Town)
- **Population:** ~3,300
- **Founded:** 1830s after Cherokee removal; named for brothers Simeon and Linzey Weaver
- **Incorporated:** 1945 (voted 60–4)
- **First school:** Cave Creek Academy, 1861
- **Railroad arrived:** 1868
- **David Weaver** (son of Linzey) donated land for post office and depot, served as postmaster and station agent
- **Almost renamed:** Weaverdale and Weaverburg floated in 1890s
- **Fort McClellan** drove population growth; town adapted after fort closed
- **Amenities:** BMX track, skate park, basketball and tennis courts, walking trail, picnic facilities, community garden
- **Chief Ladiga Trail** passes through town
- **JSU:** 6 miles away
- **City website:** weaveralabama.org
- **Weaver First UMC:** 545 Anniston Street, Weaver, AL 36277 — weaverfirstumc.org
- **Church self-description:** "a small church with a big vision: growing a garden of disciples through transformational worship, radical hospitality, and extravagant service"
- **Church partners:** Community Enabler, Interfaith Ministries, Boy and Girl Scouts
- **Church facilities:** Basketball gym, roller skating rink
