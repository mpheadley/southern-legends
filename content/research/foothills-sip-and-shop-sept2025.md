# Anniston Star / Foothills Magazine — "Six places to sip and shop this fall"
*By Misti Hayes — September 23, 2025*
*Source: annistonstar.com, Foothills magazine Fall 2025 issue (subscriber access)*

---

From the first sip of coffee to the last rays of the sunset, each of these stops offers a reason to slow down and savor the beauty of life in the Foothills.

---

## Le Coin des Artistes
This corner bistro in historic downtown Oxford marries art with appetite. With a revolving menu of specials and an eclectic atmosphere, it's the perfect spot to settle in with rich coffee at breakfast or a thoughtfully crafted lunch while lingering in the hum of inspiration. An array of art and murals make you feel as if you're sipping a cocktail (or mocktail) in an art gallery, and the locally sourced, custom stained glass window is breathtaking. Rendezvous on Thursday evenings from 6-9 p.m. with friends, or sit back solo and relish the classical and jazz music on a velvet sofa. Don't miss the French dip sandwich and white chocolate-blueberry bread pudding — they're both must-haves, and available for online orders.

**Le Coin des Artistes, 12 Choccolocco Street, Oxford, (256) 403-6451**

---

## Longleaf Bookshop and Bindery
Just a leisurely stroll from Le Coin, Longleaf Bookshop on Choccolocco Street offers a homespun vibe that delights both old and young readers. A passion project by owner Cathy Simpson and her daughter Sarah Waggoner, this mother-daughter team shares their vision of local voices and bestselling authors. Cozy reading spots abound — from a bean bag and rocking chair in the children's section (guarded by a medieval knight) to tucked-away nooks for adults. A highlight? The handmade journals and custom bookbinding services. Whether you're repairing a treasured classic or creating a family recipe book, the art of binding keeps stories alive in both paper and memory.

**Longleaf Bookshop and Bindery, 30 East Choccolocco Street, Oxford**

---

## Romarin et Corbeaux
Just a ten-minute drive away on Noble Street in downtown Anniston, our next stop at Romarin et Corbeaux welcomes visitors into its cozy blend of metaphysical shop and community hub. The first to greet you isn't always owner Aubrey but rather Von D, the shop's beloved feline "manager," who demands belly rubs before letting you browse. Inside, upholstered chairs gather near the fireplace, shelves overflow with crystals and candles, and books brim with regional lore and history (a frequent request from local customers). Schedule a massage or sign up for one of the many classes offered each month. Don't leave without a Baker's Bars soap or Vellum Street tallow lotion — and be sure to give a nod to Hades, Von D's junior partner, on your way out.

**Romarin et Corbeaux, 1020 Noble Street, Anniston**

---

## Old Noble
Step further down Noble Street to discover Old Noble, a charming shop opened by Justin Hall. Its carefully styled window displays are as inviting as what lies inside: a trove of gourmet foods, seasonal décor, and gift-able treasures. Pair their mimosa jam with one of their trays and tea towels, add Romarin's soaps and a book from Longleaf, and you've created the perfect hostess bundle or holiday gift. Justin's eye for detail and warm customer care make Old Noble a shop you'll return to again and again.

**Old Noble, 1028 Noble Street, Anniston, (256) 274-3208**

---

## Blackberry Cottage Studios
As a lover of all things rabbit, I was instantly charmed by the whimsical sign of a bunny pedaling a bicycle — it felt like an invitation to joy before I even stepped inside Blackberry Cottage. When I met the three owners — Angela, Lacy, and Julie — I wanted nothing more than to sit down over a cup of tea and watch their craft come alive. Pottery here is more than clay and glaze; it speaks to our youth, to the humbleness of handmade things, and to the delight of creating with our own hands. Visitors gather around, chatting as clay turns to art, and art becomes memory. In just a few hours, you leave with more than pottery — you leave with stories, laughter, and maybe even new friends.

I walked away with a sweet little glazed pumpkin perfect for fall, and yes … I couldn't resist a shirt with their logo. After all, bunnies on bicycles are everything.

**Blackberry Cottage Studios, 2115 Leighton Avenue, Anniston, (256) 860-0192**

---

## Iron City Bloomery
To round out the day, escape to Iron City Bloomery, a picturesque florist and flower farm just off Iron City Cutoff in Anniston. With its storybook-like shingled cottage, it overflows with seasonal blooms and ready-made arrangements for any occasion. Don't miss their pressed-flower frame classes — living art pieces to brighten your walls. Owner Katelyn's passion shows in every bouquet and workshop she hosts, and her craft classes are a perfect way to learn something new while connecting with community. It's also a lovely backdrop for photo sessions; spots fill up quickly, so contact them soon to book that holiday card photo session.

**Iron City Bloomery, 1316 Iron City Cutoff Road, Anniston, (256) 473-3332**
