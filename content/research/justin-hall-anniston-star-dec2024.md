# Anniston Star — Gift shop opens in historic downtown building
*By Bill Wilson, Star Staff Writer — December 11, 2024*
*Source: annistonstar.com (subscriber access)*

---

ANNISTON — "Uniquely Anniston" is the best way to describe the new Old Noble gift shop, which held its ribbon cutting and grand opening on the afternoon of Thursday, Dec. 5. The historic building at 1028 Noble St. was filled with merriment and mirth as holiday shoppers and friends celebrated the new business owned by Justin Hall and his fiancé, Kevin Cheatwood.

After the ribbon cutting, the cash register began to ring as a line of shoppers clutched their purchases while Hall rang them up. For Hall, opening the business was a dream he envisioned five years ago when he noticed the lack of Anniston-themed gifts in the area.

One of Hall's goals for the shop was to feature Alabama-made products, specifically items representing Anniston and other local communities.

"When I started the gift shop dream, there was nothing with Anniston in the area, so I wanted to bring that to the city, for the city to enjoy — and not just the city of Anniston, but Oxford and everyone else," he said.

On the shelves were Anniston pillows, enameled cups, dish towels inscribed with "The Real Housewives of Anniston," books about Anniston, an Anniston-themed Monopoly game and stuffed dog bones. There was also a wide variety of Alabama-themed merchandise for holiday shoppers to choose from.

Other items seen on the shelves included jams, jellies, stationary, books, toys, candles, decorative plates, Christmas decorations and much more.

Customers embraced the new gift shop, with some hugging Hall as well, as they made their purchases.

"I love it, and I love the fact that the community came out to support it, and I was able to provide such a treasure with the building as well, for the community to enjoy for many years to come," Hall said.

During the ribbon-cutting ceremony, Karla Eden, director of Main Street Anniston, highlighted the 129-year-old, three-level building's transformation.

"This building has been a part of our history, and now, thanks to your tireless efforts, it's become a beautiful part of our future," Eden said. "The time, labor and love you've poured into rehabilitating this space is nothing short of inspiring. You've not only preserved a piece of our past, but have breathed new life into it, making it a vibrant place for our community to gather and shop."

Eden added that the occasion marked the beginning of something wonderful.

"A place where memories will be made, gifts will be shared, and this historic building will continue to tell its story for generations to come," she said.

Over the years the building's tenants have included various jewelry stores, Holly's Department Store and, in the 1920s, a dance hall was located on the second floor.

Among the shoppers at Old Noble was Patty King, who was caught up in the holiday spirit.

"I think it's fantastic. I heard all the advertising, and I just decided I'd run by on my way home. It's great. You have this Christmas atmosphere — it's wonderful," King said.

Another local business owner, Ann Welch of Nunnally's Art & Frame, admired the shop's offerings and the camaraderie of the moment.

"It's wonderful. I'm so excited. It's exactly what we needed — home decor gifts — and that's perfect for downtown," Welch said.

Like others, Welch was drawn to the Alabama-made goods.

"I love all the things made in Alabama or the things with Alabama themes on them — not just the college, but the state — and also this cranberry pepper jelly," she said, gesturing to a table of hors d'oeuvres prepared for the grand opening.

"I had to put a jar of that aside," she added with a smile as she continued to shop.

Old Noble is open Monday - Sunday from 10 a.m. - 5 p.m., and can be reached at 256-274-3208.
