## The Building: 1028 Noble Street

- Built circa 1895. Three-story Victorian brick commercial structure.
- Original features: segmentally arched windows, galvanized iron cornice, 19-foot ceilings on first floor.
- Interior originally had a central open core with skylight above — later filled in on second floor, remnants visible on third floor.
- Mid-20th century (1950s–60s): aluminum facade applied directly over original 1895 brick — building's history literally buried under modernization.
- Reportedly a jewelry store for a significant portion of its mid-century history, consistent with neighboring Rosa Lee Jewelry (11 E 10th St).
- Source: National Register of Historic Places documentation (NPGallery, NPS).

## The Name

The "Old Noble" name is likely a conscious nod to the historic Old Noble movie theater — positioning the shop as a cultural successor to a lost Anniston institution. (NYT 1976 archive references the "Old Noble and Cameo movie theaters" as cultural touchstones.)
