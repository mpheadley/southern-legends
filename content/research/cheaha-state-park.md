# Cheaha State Park — Research Notes

## Facts
- **Full name:** Cheaha State Park
- **Location:** 19644 Highway 281, Delta, AL 36258 (Clay and Cleburne counties)
- **Category:** Places
- **Elevation:** 2,407 feet — highest point in Alabama
- **Acreage:** 2,799 acres
- **Opened:** 1933 — Alabama's oldest continuously operating state park
- **Built by:** Civilian Conservation Corps (CCC), 1933-1939
- **Key structures:** Bunker Tower, stone cabins, lake, hiking trails — all hand-built from local stone
- **Trails:** Bald Rock (0.3 mi, wheelchair-accessible boardwalk), Pulpit Rock (short/steep, sunset spot), Cave Creek (3.3 mi, serious elevation, Cheaha Wilderness), Mountain Laurel (stream/laurel thickets to Rock Garden Outlook)
- **Trail connection:** Cheaha Trailhead of the Pinhoti Trail, which connects to the Appalachian Trail in Georgia
- **Access road:** Talladega Scenic Drive (Highway 281) through Talladega National Forest
- **Admission:** $2-$5 per person (military free with ID)
- **Amenities:** Lodging (stone cabins, chalets), camping, restaurant at summit
- **Website:** alapark.com/parks/cheaha-state-park
- **Drive time from Matt:** ~40 minutes from Pleasant Valley/Jacksonville
- **Position:** Roughly halfway between Birmingham and Atlanta
