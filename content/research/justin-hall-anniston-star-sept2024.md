# Anniston Star — New gift shop revitalizes historic Noble Street property
*By Bill Wilson, Star Staff Writer — September 24, 2024*
*Source: annistonstar.com (subscriber access)*
*Published before opening — pre-announcement piece*

---

Old Noble, a new gift shop will be opening its doors in the old Griffin's Jewelers building in time for the upcoming holiday shopping season. Justin Hall, who co-owns the building at 1028 Noble Street, said he hopes to open the gift shop by November 1st but no later than Black Friday.

Hall said the gift shop will fill a void in downtown Anniston ever since the Rabbit Hutch closed; and like the Rabbit Hutch, Old Noble will offer made-in-Alabama merchandise.

"It will have a lot of Alabama based goods. I've tried to search for things local throughout the state as I travel with my job," Hall said. "It will have local things that people have probably been missing since the Rabbit Hutch closed."

Hall said the gift shop will carry jams, candy, books, toys, jellies, candles, pillows, stationery items along with apparel for all ages and gift baskets.

Hall said he hopes the gift shop will get people back downtown no matter what one's budget is.

"I'm excited about it because I wanted my gift shop to be for everyone, where you feel comfortable," he said.

Hall and his fiance bought the 129 year-old three-level building back in 2021.

"11,000 square feet of possibilities," Hall said about the former jewelry store that's currently undergoing renovations.

"I knew from the get-go that I wanted it to be a gift shop because I know every small town I've ever been to has some sort of little gift shop and obviously the Rabbit Hutch was a historical gift shop downtown for many years," Hall said.

Karla Eden, Main Street Anniston director, said the revitalization of historic downtown properties preserves the character and history of the community while breathing new life into the area.

"I'm excited to see the energy shift as more buildings are restored and prepared for retail, bringing new opportunities and vibrancy to the heart of Downtown Anniston," Eden said, "Old Noble is set to become a staple in our community and I look forward to supporting their business for years to come as they contribute to the continued growth and vitality of our historic district."

Hall said there is a lot of economic potential in downtown Anniston with established events like Noble Street Festival along with the future economic impact from the completion of the nearby Chief Ladiga Trail extension.

"I saw it as a great opportunity to invest in the business," he said.

Hall, a Main Street Anniston board of directors member, said the future of Noble Street has no limits.

"The sky's the limit and the reason I say that is I see so much energy down here. I will tell you, when I first moved here, I jumped right in. I've always been a mover and a shaker and so I jumped right in, joined Main Street Anniston and have been part of helping clean up downtown to bring that positive energy to this area," Hall said.

"It's like a domino effect, when people start seeing one building renovated it starts to bring more excitement into the area. In the little bit of time I've been in Anniston, there's been such a positive change in cleaning up downtown," he said.

"I think that is because ever since the pandemic, they love that nostalgia of going into your hometown. I've always said your main street is basically the artery to your city and a lot of people want that nostalgia to come back and support a small business," he said.

Hall said people want to support "mom and pop" shops instead of patronizing big box retail outlets.

---

## The Grand Staircase and the Hidden Facade

For years the building had a lackluster shell masking a treasure inside. In 2010 the sheath was removed revealing majestic upper floor windows.

"We're having a whole exterior facade renovation. There's old cast iron back there, there were terracotta window frames at one time," Hall said.

Hall said the contractor will soon start boarding up the front of the building as part of the restoration process. Once completed the front of the building will go back to its original look, Hall said.

Hall said that over the years the building's tenants have included various jewelry stores, Holly's Department Store, and other businesses.

"I also learned that there was a dance hall in the 20s that they had on the second floor," Hall said. At one time the building included the largest skylights in the city. Sunlight spills through the front windows on the two upper levels illuminating a vast expanse of vintage oak flooring that will soon be converted into living quarters.

One of the hidden gems that's been kept from public view for decades is a grand staircase on the second level. Hall said the majestic staircase used to go to the ground floor when the building was Holly's Department Store.

"It's just a magnificent piece of history here in Anniston a lot of people don't know about," Hall said. The oak staircase has 15 steps with very decorative newel posts and balusters.

---

## No Place Like Anniston

When Hall got off work during the pandemic, he would walk through downtown Anniston taking in its charm and beautiful architecture of the old buildings.

"And with my current position, I travel all over the state of Alabama, and there was no other area in the state that had the beauty in architecture of the buildings like Anniston did," Hall said, "I was part of a revitalization of Decatur and I thought, you know, there was a wonderful possibility of what could happen here and lo and behold, got engaged and moved to Calhoun County and bought the building and here we are."
