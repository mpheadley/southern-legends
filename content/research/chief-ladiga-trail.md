# Chief Ladiga Trail — Research Notes

## Facts
- **Full name:** Chief Ladiga Trail
- **Location:** Anniston to Alabama-Georgia state line (passes through Weaver, Jacksonville, Piedmont)
- **Category:** Places
- **Counties:** Calhoun County, Cleburne County
- **Length:** Nearly 40 miles (Alabama's first and longest rail trail)
- **Surface:** Asphalt, mostly gentle grade, ~700 ft elevation gain heading east
- **Combined route:** Connects to Silver Comet Trail in Georgia — 105+ miles total to suburban Atlanta, longest paved pedestrian pathway in the US
- **Origin:** 1990, City of Piedmont received Land and Water Conservation Grant to purchase unused rail corridor
- **Georgia connection:** 2008
- **Hall of Fame:** Rails to Trails Conservancy Hall of Fame, inducted 2009
- **2025 extension:** 6.5-mile Anniston extension to Amtrak depot (April 2025)
- **Economic impact:** JSU Center for Economic Development study — $9.7 million in total economic output (2023) across four Calhoun County trails
- **Tourism director:** Audrey Maxwell, Calhoun County Area Chamber and Visitors Center
- **Steepest section:** Piedmont to state line, 680 ft to 1,000 ft over 9 miles
- **Uses:** Walking, running, cycling, inline skating; equestrians in Cleburne County
- **Trail town businesses (Piedmont):**
  - Pinhoti Pizza Company — brick oven pizza, at trail/Main Street intersection
  - Elevated Grounds — coffee shop steps from trail
  - Ladiga Trail Trikes — Neal Gladden, second location (also runs Rocket City Trikes in Huntsville), recumbent trikes
  - Main Street South — 10,000 sq ft vendor mall/mercantile, Eubanks Welcome Center
- **Websites:** piedmontcity.org, traillink.com, downtownpiedmont.com
