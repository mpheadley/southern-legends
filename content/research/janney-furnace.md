# Janney Furnace — Research Notes

## Facts
- **Full name:** Janney Furnace
- **Location:** Off Highway 77, Ohatchee, AL, Calhoun County
- **Category:** Places (historical)
- **Built:** 1863-64
- **Builder:** Alfred A. Janney, Montgomery foundry owner (specialized in Confederate ordnance — cannons, shot)
- **Purpose:** Produce pig iron for Confederate war effort
- **Construction:** Enslaved stonecutters hand-shaped sandstone blocks (some over a ton), stacked 50 feet high
- **Design capacity:** 15 tons of cast iron per day
- **Site features:** Workers excavated into south side of ridge, built retaining wall, leveled hilltop for stocking yard
- **Logistics:** Coosa River nearby for shipping at high water; wagon to railroad terminus 16 miles away in Anniston when river low
- **Destroyed:** July 14, 1864 by Union cavalry under Major General Lovell H. Rousseau (2,300 men)
- **Destroyer detail:** Engineer Officer Ed Ruger's detail; hit Cane Creek Furnace first, then Janney
- **Result:** Never produced a single pound of pig iron — destroyed before firing
- **Previous ironworks:** Cane Creek Furnace (operated since 1840) was in the area; Janney initially bought pig iron from it
- **Post-war:** Janney salvaged equipment (pulleys, wheels, boilers) but never rebuilt; war ended less than a year later
- **What remains:** 50-foot sandstone stack still standing
- **Park:** Janney Furnace Park, maintained by Calhoun County Parks and Recreation Office
- **Museums:** Confederate Museum and Native American Museum (500+ artifacts) on site
- **National Register:** Listed since 1976
- **County name note:** Calhoun County was still called Benton County in 1863
- **Website:** janneyfurnace.org
