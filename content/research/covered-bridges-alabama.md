# Alabama's Covered Bridges — Research Notes

## Facts
- **Category:** Places
- **Location:** Northeast Alabama (Oxford/Calhoun County) and Blount County

### Coldwater Covered Bridge (Oxford)
- **Location:** Oxford Lake Park, off State Route 21, Oxford, AL
- **Built:** ~1850 (some sources say 1839)
- **Builder:** A formerly enslaved man, possibly a forerunner of Horace King
- **Span:** 63 feet
- **Truss type:** Rare combination of Multiple King-post truss and Town Lattice
- **Original location:** Over Coldwater Creek on Airport Road, near old Hughes Mill
- **History:** Partially burned 1920, survived; carried motor vehicles until 1974; moved to Oxford Lake Park 1990
- **National Register:** Since 1973
- **Maintained by:** City of Oxford

### Horton Mill Bridge (Blount County)
- **Built:** Current structure 1934 (original 1894)
- **Height:** 70 feet above Calvert Prong of Little Warrior River — highest covered bridge above water in the United States
- **Length:** 220 feet
- **Truss:** Town Lattice
- **Builder:** 15-man crew led by Talmedge Horton (family descendant), 1.5 years
- **National Register:** 1970 — first covered bridge in southeastern US added
- **Status:** Closed to motor vehicles 2022, open to pedestrians

### Swann Bridge (Blount County)
- **Also called:** Joy Covered Bridge
- **Built:** 1933
- **Length:** 324 feet across Locust Fork of Black Warrior River — longest covered bridge in Alabama (after 385-ft Nectar Bridge burned 1993)
- **Construction:** Three spans, Town Lattice truss, wood and metal combination
- **National Register:** 1981
- **Status:** Closed for repairs, reopened to motor vehicles 2012

### Easley Bridge (Blount County)
- **Built:** 1927
- **Length:** 95 feet across Dub Branch
- **Builder:** Crew led by foreman Forrest Tidwell and nephew Zelma
- **Status:** Only covered bridge in Blount County still open to vehicles

### General
- Blount County: unofficial "Covered Bridge Capital of Alabama"
- Blount County Covered Bridge Festival: held annually in October
- Security cameras installed at all three Blount County bridges in 2015 (after Easley vandalism/graffiti)
- Driving map: northalabama.org
- Blount County trail accessible from State Route 75 north of Oneonta
