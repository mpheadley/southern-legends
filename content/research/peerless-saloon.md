# The Peerless Saloon & Grille — Research Notes

## Facts
- **Name:** The Peerless Saloon & Grille
- **Address:** 15 West 10th Street, Anniston, AL 36201
- **Website:** peerlesssaloonandgrill.com
- **Established:** 1899 by Robert E. "Daddy" Garner (came from a farm in Georgia)
- **Distinction:** Alabama's oldest operating bar
- **National Register:** Listed on National Register of Historic Places (1985)
- **Restored/reopened:** 1992

## History
- Liquor became legal in Anniston 1890; Garner opened The Peerless 1899
- Classic Revival Gothic building
- House blend whiskey: "Old Wildcat"
- Garner bought old church pews, sawed them in half for customer booths
- **Mahogany bar:** Purchased at 1904 St. Louis World's Fair, shipped to Anniston 1905 (reportedly on same train as Vulcan statue to Birmingham). Mirror-backed, still in the "Alabama Room"
- **Second floor brothel:** Four rooms with ornamented fireplaces + fifth bedroom in loft via ladder
- **Lucinda Talley:** Madam, ran operation from chair at top of stairs. Screened visitors, collected payment. Known for carrying a gun, gifting expensive ties at Christmas. Ran it 20+ years.
- **Shooting:** ~1919–1920, police chased suspect into building, officer fired, missed suspect, killed Lucinda at top of stairs
- **Ghost reports:** Staff/owners report seeing Lucinda at her post, footsteps on second floor, glasses breaking behind bar
- Alabama went dry statewide 1915; Garner apparently bootlegged until death 1919 (age 63)
- Decades of abandonment followed
- **Netflix:** Scenes from *Devil All the Time* filmed inside (Bill Skarsgard, Sebastian Stan)
