# Easter — Two Years
*Journal entry — raw material*

---

## Matt's words

### Last Easter

Last Easter I was manic and mad. I went to the church in Anniston — the one where I used to work. I was dressed in purple pants and a pink tie. I took tons of selfies. I felt something — anger, exhilaration, grief, I'm not sure.

---

### This Easter

This Easter was our second Sunday visiting Jacksonville First United Methodist Church. Andy — the pastor, a former friend from years ago when I was at Weaver First UMC — invited me to help serve communion.

It was a meaningful moment. After months of not attending church at all. After feeling like I had lost my faith. After wondering if reconstruction was even possible, let alone beginning.

---

## Notes
- These only mean something next to each other. Publish as one piece with a visible break between them — white space, a divider, a year passing.
- The selfies: a manic person documenting themselves, wanting to be seen a certain way. Contrast with the quietness of serving communion.
- Andy and Matt have history — former friends from Weaver First UMC days. The invitation wasn't from a stranger.
- "Help serve communion" — for a former pastor, that's loaded. Being trusted with something again.
- "Reconstruction" — theological weight. Faith deconstruction/reconstruction is a known arc for people who've left or lost church. Worth keeping that word.
- The church visits to First Anniston (heartbreak, anger) may be woven in as context for last Easter, or stay in their own file.
