# The Manic Episode
*Journal entry — raw material*

---

## Matt's words

After Ash Wednesday, I resigned abruptly from my role at the church as Associate Pastor. I felt angry, but exhilarated. It was messy. I started posting on social media about it, and doubled down on the flower farm. Felt wildly confident.

Then started spending money, scaling up. Ideas came flooding. I had multiple projects and continued adding. A perimeter fence for the farm. Workshop upgrades. A Ladiga trail shop and kiosk. Power supply visits from Alabama Power. I met with the Economic Developer, Toby Bennington. Mayor Ciara Smith visited our farm. She saw the vision. BNI people were excited. Everyone was excited, except Heather. She was overwhelmed.

We were doing 2-3 farmers markets, pop ups, graduations, weddings, dance recitals, theater bouquets for Cast Kids. We were everywhere. I was working 16-20 hour days, sleeping 3-4 hours a night, if that. I felt everything was holy and amazing. My prayer life felt on fire. My family was holding on for dear life.

Heather suspected something might be off. My friends were joking about "manic matt." I would go out for a 30 minute errand and be gone all day, passing out business cards.

I was dozing off driving 10 minutes to Walmart. This was back in February or early March.

## Notes
- This is the thing that happened before the crash — the episode that preceded the diagnosis, the farm loss, the hospitalization
- The farm scaling felt holy at the time. That's important — it wasn't reckless from the inside. It felt like calling.
- "manic matt" — his friends named it before he could
- The dozing while driving is a detail that shows how far gone it was physically, not just mentally
- Heather was the first to suspect. She was overwhelmed while he felt everything was amazing. That gap is worth writing into.
- During the manic episode, after resigning from the church, Matt believed his former boss Tammy was going to shoot him and his family. He told his therapist. She recognized it as paranoia — he hadn't been diagnosed bipolar yet. This was a psychotic feature of the manic episode, not something he believed when well.
- **Naming decision needed before publishing:** Tammy is a real person. Naming her in connection with a paranoid delusion requires deciding whether to use her name, a pseudonym, or no name at all. This is the kind of detail that could hurt someone who reads it, even with full context. Decide before writing this entry.
- After the hospital, Tammy texted once to check on him. That's the full extent of contact. The paranoia and the single text are part of the same story.
