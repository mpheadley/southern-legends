# Becoming a Web Designer
*Journal entry — raw material*

---

## Matt's words

I took weeks continuing to apply for jobs. Then I started to give up. I played solitaire a lot at my parents' while living in their camper. I found myself continuing to distract myself that way. Then I signed up for a coding class online. I began to feel drawn to web design. I started feeling excited to wake up in the morning.

Around February, I drove to Called Coffee for the first time since probably June of the year before. I sat in my car for thirty minutes just trying to figure out how long I was gonna sit in Called Coffee and who I would talk to and what I would say. I avoided going out for weeks after that. Wanted to, but was afraid. I felt like I had disappeared.

After getting my own website launched, I started making cold calls. I got hung up on a lot. Nobody wanted anything, except for one or two service companies. They ghosted me. I was asking friends for referrals. Finally got a friend whose mother wanted a site for her business (Between Worlds, Giovanna Minotta).

## Notes
- Solitaire → coding class → February visit to Called Coffee (alone, thirty minutes in the car) → cold calls → first client. That arc is the piece.
- The February Called Coffee visit is the physical version of the isolation — wanting to go out, afraid to, sitting in the car. The body was part of it too: weight gained, torn meniscus, bad knee and ankle. Worth one sentence, not explained.
- "I started feeling excited to wake up in the morning" — that's the turn. Simple. Don't over-explain it.
- Getting hung up on. Nobody wanted it. Then one yes.
- Between Worlds / Giovanna Minotta was the first real client
