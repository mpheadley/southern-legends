# The Profiles I Didn't Finish
*Concept note — not a draft yet.*

---

## The idea
Some of the profiles in `content/drafts/` will never become profiles. The story of why is sometimes more interesting than the finished piece would have been.

What happens when you sit down to write about someone and stop? What did you find when you got close? That's not a failed profile — it's a journal entry about what this work actually is.

## Known candidates
- **Lewis Downing** (`drafts/lewis-downing-draft.md`) — draft started April 2026, stalled. What stopped it? What was the scene, the moment, the thing that didn't resolve?
- **Matt Headley** — a draft profile of himself that never worked. The profile format is designed to observe someone from the outside. Turning it on yourself collapses. The meta-piece (`the-same-domain.md`) is the self-portrait instead.

## Questions to answer before writing
For each stalled profile:
- What was the last thing written?
- What was the thing that couldn't be figured out?
- Is the subject still someone worth writing about — just not yet?
- Or is the story of the attempt the actual story?

## What this piece is NOT
A list of excuses for unfinished work.

## What it IS
An honest look at what the profiles cost — not just time, but proximity. Getting close enough to write about someone sometimes reveals something that doesn't belong in a profile. That's worth writing about.

## Format
Probably not one unified piece — more likely individual short journal entries as each stalled profile gets examined. Could be a series: "Lewis," "Myself," etc.

## When to write this
After `the-same-domain.md` is published. That piece establishes the editorial honesty. This one builds on it.
