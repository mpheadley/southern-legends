# Seed: Cropwell

**Working title options:** "An Hour Away" / "Cropwell" / "The Distance"

**Emotional core:**
The most invisible he felt wasn't the hospital. It was the month in Cropwell — his parents' camper, an hour from Calhoun County — while Heather and the kids were at her parents' house. Her parents were afraid he might be dangerous. He wasn't. He was sick. The geography of that fear is the sharpest edge of the whole chapter.

---

## What this piece is about

Separation during a psychiatric hospitalization — not the hospitalization itself, but what happened after. The family split in two directions. Heather holding the kids and explaining something she couldn't fully explain. Matt at his parents' in Cropwell, an hour south, invisible not because he disappeared but because fear moved his family away from him.

The in-law fear is not the villain of the piece. It's the human response to something they didn't understand and weren't equipped for. That's the honest tension: it was understandable, and it was devastating.

## The line that earns this piece

*I felt most invisible living an hour away from Calhoun County. Not in the hospital. In Cropwell.*

---

## Tension to hold

This isn't a piece about blaming the in-laws. It's about how fear travels through a family during a crisis — and lands hardest on the person the fear is about. The question isn't whether their fear was understandable. It was. The question is what it cost Matt and Heather, and whether naming it does something true.
