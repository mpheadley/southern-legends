# Seed: Community & Rootedness

**Cut from:** `the-same-domain.mdx` (line 15 comment)

**Seed line:**
> "I think starting my business, unknowingly, drew on and reclaimed my community that I've been building, all these years that I grew up and worked here."

---

## What this piece is about

Building something in the place you grew up. The community that accumulates over a life — church, school, farm patrons, neighbors, ministry relationships — and how that web becomes visible when you need it, or when you start building something new.

Not a nostalgia piece. More like: I didn't realize I had roots here until I started pulling on them.

---

## Possible angles

- The Chamber of Commerce meetup (Called Coffee) — people who knew Matt from the flower farm, from church, from school. They showed up in a room without being summoned.
- Southern Legends itself as an act of rootedness — profiling people here, not elsewhere. Why here? Because this is where I am. Because I know these people.
- The difference between community you build intentionally and community that accrues without your noticing.

---

## Tension to hold

This isn't a triumphant "my community carried me" piece. The hospital chapter is real — Jason called weekly, Tammy texted once, that was the whole of it. The community was quieter than expected when it mattered most. That's part of the honest telling.

---

## When to write it

When Matt has more distance from the immediate re-entry. This is a reflective piece, not a crisis piece. It requires some stability to write well.
