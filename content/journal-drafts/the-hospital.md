# The Hospital
*Journal entry — raw material*

---

## Matt's words

On a trip to the dump with my Dad, he told me it was time for me to go to the hospital. He didn't want his grandkids to lose their dad.

After being persuaded by Kyle — he had a very positive experience, had preached about it earlier that year that it was like a "vacation with grippy socks" — reluctantly, I consented to Heather and my Dad taking me to the hospital.

We drove to Birmingham, walked to UAB. I was there with them waiting in the ER for 6 hours. When the psychiatrist asked me if I wanted to be admitted to the residential program, I said I did not. They let me go, and I felt happy and excited, strangely, to be going home.

The next day, I asked to go back. Dad took me.

I was in the hospital for 10 days. (I was sure it was 3 weeks.)

I spent Halloween in the hospital.

When I got out, I had to live in my parents' camper for a month while Heather and the kids lived with her parents.

Heather got a job at the museum in November while I was in the hospital.

When I got out I moved into the trailer in November with my kids after the power and water got turned on, and continued applying for jobs.

We got to spend Thanksgiving and Christmas together.

## After — Kyle, Heather (raw, April 18 2026, 6am)

Kyle and i met for coffee yesterday. I told him i was in there 3 weeks. It was awkward, but good. I told him i was angry with him when i was in the hospital. He told me he thought i should go, when he came to see me before i went. I told him it was not at all a "grippy sock vacation" like he had experienced.

Talking with Heather about it later that evening, outside under our sweetgum tree at dinner as the sun was setting. We talked about the hospital visit, my coffee with Kyle earlier that morning. she was suprised it was 3 weeks. then when i woke up at 5:50am to journal, and read through my journal from the hospital that i had found yesterday, a notebook stashed away in my bedside table drawer, and read it. i saw the dates. i had fewer than i thought. i double checked this morning on my google timeline. it was Oct 25 through Nov 4. i missed halloween with heather and friend and the kids on glenwood terrace, our annual tradition. and i missed the house sale. heather had to come out to visit me 3 times during my time there. once to see me. once to get the notary on staff to notarize our house closure papers and get my signature. and a second time because the notary on staff wasn't there so we had to hire a mobile notary.

on the visits, we sat akwardly where the staff could see us, at the table in the main room where we spent all our time eating, playing cards, dominos, watcing tv while others . we talked quitely, time limit was 30 minutes. others who didin't have visitors had to stay in their rooms. it was quiet time.

She asked me if i thought it was good for me, in hindsight. I said it was. it was and it wasn't.
She said she needed me to be gone. She couldn't take anymore. I was fighting her on the move. On selling our stuff from the farm. I said it was a good place to get me away from everything. And i hated it then, but now i have a story. Not many people know what its' like to be in a loony house. Kyle asked me if I met anyone there that ate oatmeal with their hands. I said, no. My best funny story was of me and my comrades playing badminton in a little gym in our hospital gowns and grippy socks. I was frustrated that the resident psychiatrist criticized my psychiatrist's approach of cycling me through meds before getting to the full maximum dosage on any of them. It was bewildering, not knowing who was right. They started me back on seroquel when i got there, a drug i had discontinued before. But they would see results faster on seroquel than on what had just started, lamotrigine. And the point of the 4th floor (west?) was to get you out as soon as you are stable. So eventually, after the first 10 days or so, i realized id have to start telling them what they wanted to hear, to get out of there. That i wasn't feeling as suicidal.
They did a good job. They were mostly compassionate, except some of the techs (?what was their role?) that were paternalistic and patronizing.
I found my journal about the whole thing. I'll take a look.

## Inside — ten days (raw, April 17 2026, 4am)

its a cheap brown notebook with lined paper. i wrote in it with the only pencil i had, the half length one they gave me because apparently it was less dangerous than a full length pencil. i remember the grippy socks. Jack swindle gave me his phonen umber before i left — it was against the rules for patients / residents to share contact info. he wrote it on a scrap as i was packing my things to leave. sharondola. the indian girl that was kind and was also bipolar. the carrot cake. the ones who didin't want to be there, screaming. the other side of the floor that was for older patients. the guy who saw stuff, peple that talked to him. the ones who talked to themselfves. the half-wits. i remember all of them. feeling stuck, like i was trapped, walking in circles, literally. the shower that dribbled water. i had to huddle in the corner under that drippy thing to get wet. the yoga mat that called me. the snack time - pb crackers. waking up in the middle of the night. trying to get my seqouel dialed in, a drug i had already been on. the way the psychiatrist in charge critiziced my psychaitrists method of cycling through so many different drugs withotu getting to maximum dosage before switching.

i remember calling heather on the phone, and getting calls, while in the psych ward.
reading the butlerian jihad, the late prequel in the dune series. talking with one of the attending psychaitrists about it, and Jack, who had both read dune and some others. then not being able to read dune when i got home, even though i asked them to bring it to me while in the psych ward because i enjoyed the jihad.

the carrot cake - dialing that in, figuriong out from the others that you had to write it in on the meal card. and how they often got the meal cards wrong - you wrote yours in and hoped for the best, or that there might be an extra plate with what you wanted on it.

the freeing feeling of walking out of the ward to try to find a notary, in vein, with heather and the social worker.

going to group therapy 2x / day, feeling so angry, sad, liike it was a waste of time.
the funny experience of going to the gym that i wrote about elsewhere, playing badminton . walking on a treadmiill watching the office, then getting sore because i had been so sedentary.

the fall festival. stiiing in a corner doodling because it reminded me of my kids. all the adults going around to festival stations, craft tables, getting candy, the staff dressed up as farmers, aliens

halloween - walking the streets with thousands of parents and their kids. drinking spiked cider in my yeti. watching the kids run to doors. admiirng the neighborhoods extravagant werewolves and vampires and spider webs, homeowners sitting on their doorsepts handing out fistfuls of candy.

jack wasn't supposed to give me his number. i lost his number, the scrap of paper he wrote it on. i'm still sad about that.

my counseilng sessions with sam the woman with the muslim — what's it called? a hijab. — were the most helpful. she said the move was triggering me. my history with my dad. having to move as a kid because of choices he made. and now the same thing i had worked so hard to avoid was happening to me and my family.
