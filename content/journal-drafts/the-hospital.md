# The Hospital
*Journal entry — raw material*

---

## Matt's words

On a trip to the dump with my Dad, he told me it was time for me to go to the hospital. He didn't want his grandkids to lose their dad.

After being persuaded by Kyle — he had a very positive experience, had preached about it earlier that year that it was like a "vacation with grippy socks" — reluctantly, I consented to Heather and my Dad taking me to the hospital.

We drove to Birmingham, walked to UAB. I was there with them waiting in the ER for 6 hours. When the psychiatrist asked me if I wanted to be admitted to the residential program, I said I did not. They let me go, and I felt happy and excited, strangely, to be going home.

The next day, I asked to go back. Dad took me.

I was in the hospital for 3 weeks.

While I was inside, we signed the papers to sell the house. A mobile notary came to the psychiatric center. Heather drove out to UAB to meet me there so we could sign together.

---

## Notes
- Kyle's "vacation with grippy socks" sermon — the thing that made Matt willing to go. May be its own entry or woven into this one.
- The refusal, then asking to go back the next day — that turn is the emotional center of this piece
- Three weeks inside — what was that like? Not written yet.
- Dad at the dump: "He didn't want his grandkids to lose their dad." That line carries a lot.
- Signing the house sale papers from inside the psych ward — mobile notary, Heather driving to UAB to meet him. The life outside did not pause. The farm was being sold while he was locked in.
