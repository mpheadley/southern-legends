## The core tension (in plain terms)
SL was built to get web design clients. It became the place where the hardest years of Matt's life live publicly. Those two things are on the same domain. That wasn't a mistake — it was a decision made under real constraints (20+ Patreon followers already pointed here). But it's a decision that has real costs.

The specific costs:
- The psilocybin piece can't live here. (A choice involving a Schedule I substance in Alabama, in a conservative small business market.)
- The psych ward pieces can. Probably. But they're a judgment call, one at a time.
- The farm loss, the music, the ministry — those are assets here. They make the profiles feel earned.
- The meta-question: what do you do when you need to process publicly and the only platform you've built points toward clients?
