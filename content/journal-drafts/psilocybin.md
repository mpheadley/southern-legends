## What this piece is about

Matt did a psilocybin session. His brother Justin was present. Dave Barnhart — clergy colleague, licensed counselor, and participant in the Johns Hopkins University study on psilocybin and clergy — trip-sat. Barnhart framed it as harm reduction. He wasn't there as a guide in a formal capacity; he was there as a friend who knew what he was doing.

The Johns Hopkins context matters: Barnhart isn't someone who stumbled into this. He was part of a serious, peer-reviewed research program studying psilocybin's effects on clergy and religious experience. That's the frame that makes this more than a recreational story — it's a story about mental health, faith, medicine, and the edges of what's sanctioned.
