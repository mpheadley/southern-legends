## What this piece is about

The full arc of Matt's relationship with AI as a thinking/processing tool — three distinct phases, each with a different purpose and different risk.

## Matt's exact words (the arc)

> "When I was manic, I spent a lot of time chatting with AI, and I'm pretty sure that it exacerbated my mania and delusion and grandiose thinking."

> "Then I've been dumping on AI for a long time just as a way to process my thoughts while I was depressed."

> "Now I'm doing it to help me write. I'm also using voice memos."

> "I was using ChatGPT as a guide or planner when I was manic, and it encouraged me in that manner."

> "There's a whole phenomena happening right now — how people are discovering that their mental health was exacerbated or made worse by long sessions with AI chat."

> "I've been dumping on ChatGPT as like an in-between therapist."

> "Augmented journaling."

## The three phases

1. **Mania** — AI as amplifier. Grandiose thinking reinforced, not checked.
2. **Depression** — AI as void to dump into. Processing without dialogue.
3. **Now** — AI as thinking partner for actual creative work. Trying to be intentional.

## The tension

Not a cautionary tale, not a celebration. Honest: I've had a fraught relationship with this tool and I'm learning to use it differently. The question underneath: when am I thinking *with* Claude vs. letting Claude think *for* me?

## What public processing actually does

The day after "The Same Domain" published, Amber Simpson (Jacksonville artist, Bipolar 2 diagnosis, also a pastor's-world person) sent a message: *"I want to share that I am sending you every ounce of love and prayer I can muster... please know you're not alone in this ministry + mental health + small business combo."* She'd written publicly about her own diagnosis in 2023, hadn't said anything to Matt about it until he published.

That's the thing AI can't do. Processing with Claude is useful for drafting and thinking. It doesn't pull Amber out of the woodwork. The public part does that. The piece is partly about that distinction — AI as tool for making the thing, humans as what the thing reaches.
