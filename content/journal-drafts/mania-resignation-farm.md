# Post Seed — Mania, Resignation, Farm
*Captured April 15 2026.*

---

## What this piece is about

The causal chain that collapsed everything — told plainly, in sequence. Mania felt like calling. The resignation felt like obedience. The farm felt inevitable. And then the sequence ran out.

This is the piece where the structural explanation lives: not just "things fell apart" but *why* and *in what order*.

## The spine

1. The mania that felt like recovery — energy, clarity, holy confidence
2. The resignation from the church — made during the manic episode, felt like calling, wasn't
3. Doubling down on the farm — 16-20 hour days, sleeping 3-4 hours, Heather holding on
4. The diagnosis — bipolar, after the fact, naming what had already happened
5. Heather's surgery — scheduled after the manic season, now arriving into the collapse
6. The farm sale — the resignation ended the paycheck; the paycheck was the farm's lifeline
7. The hospital

## Why it belongs in its own piece

"I'm Not Going to Disappear" moves fast through the collapse on purpose — diagnosis, farm, hospital, hiding. It's a reintroduction, not an explanation. Adding this chain there would slow it down and shift the piece's purpose.

"A Letter Never Sent" implies it ("decisions I wouldn't have made in a healthier state of mind... financial consequences") but doesn't say it plainly.

This piece says it plainly.

## The tension

Not self-pity. Not explanation as excuse. The mania felt real — it felt like the most alive and called he'd ever been. That's the honest part. The collapse wasn't a failure of character. It was a sequence.
