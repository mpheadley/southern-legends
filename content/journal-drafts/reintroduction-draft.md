I thought about messaging you personally. Many times. I even drafted something- a full letter, trying to process it all. The farm sale. The bipolar diagnosis. A ministry career ending.

I thought I was disappearing. I'm not going to disappear.

---

The year I turned 40, I got severly depressed, then I got better. [Wrote about it in the Anniston Star.](/journal/hope-in-the-wilderness) Tied a bow on it. Even got a printed copy to frame in my office.

The next year, I resigned from the church after Ash Wednesday. Abruptly, angrily, with a confidence that felt like calling. I doubled down on the flower farm. I was working 16-20 hour days, sleeping three or four hours a night. Everything felt holy. Heather was trying to keep up with the whirlwind.

Then the depression came back. The bipolar diagnosis. Heather's surgery — we drove to Atlanta. We came home to a farm we couldn't keep. I stopped going outside. I was frozen. Then the hospital.

A lot happened between that framed copy and now. I'll write about it. But this isn't that post.

I didn't reach out to anyone. Jason Wright called to check on me. Tammy, my former boss, texted once after I got out of the hospital. That was the whole of it.

---

Last week I went to a Chamber of Commerce meetup at Called Coffee. After a few conversations I sat down to take a break. A woman across the room waved. Sarah. My wife's boss. A flower patron from years ago. I walked over. Karla Eden and a couple of others were debating who I was.

"That's Matt from the flower farm."

They said it was sad that it ended. I said I was still sad too.

Three coffee meetups this week. That was unthinkable two months ago.
