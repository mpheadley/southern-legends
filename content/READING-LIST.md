# Reading List

Curated recommendations that fit what Southern Legends is trying to do — attention to place, honest reckoning with faith and loss, longform profile craft. Reference material, not shipping content.

## Novels

**Wendell Berry — *Jayber Crow* or *Hannah Coulter***
Slow novels about staying in a place, losing ground, and what it means to pay attention to people. The Southern Legends impulse is basically Berry's impulse rendered in fiction.

**Padgett Powell — *Edisto***
Short Southern novel. Funny, strange, well-made. For lighter reading that still rewards close attention.

## Essays & Memoir

**Christian Wiman — *My Bright Abyss***
Former editor of *Poetry* magazine writing fragmented essays about faith after a cancer diagnosis. Honest about doubt without being corrosive. One of the few books that doesn't flinch for someone rebuilding from inside ministry grief.

**John Jeremiah Sullivan — *Pulphead***
Essays from one of the best working American longform writers. "Upon This Rock" — on a Christian rock festival — may be the single best piece written about evangelical subculture from the outside-but-sympathetic. His profiles are a masterclass for sharpening SL's craft.
