# Justin Hall — Old Noble — Interview Prep

## Background
- Co-owner of Old Noble (with Kevin Cheatwood, his fiancé), 1028 Noble Street, Anniston, AL
- Gift shop focused on Anniston-themed and Alabama-made goods. Stocks books, home decor, candles, jams, "The Real Housewives of Anniston" dish towels, Anniston Monopoly, stuffed dog bones, and more.
- Building: 1895 Victorian brick, 19-foot ceilings, aluminum 1950s facade over original brick. Previously housed jewelry stores, Holly's Department Store, and a dance hall on the second floor in the 1920s.
- Name likely nods to the historic Old Noble movie theater — a lost cultural institution.
- 5.3K Facebook followers — entire digital presence is the FB page. No website.
- Warm acquaintance from the flower farm — hosted bloom bar outside on Mother's Day, also sold flowers in-shop.
- Former florist, Huntsville area.
- **Dream started ~5 years before opening** — noticed the absence of Anniston-themed gifts around 2019.
- **Bought the building in 2021** with Kevin — three years of renovation before opening
- **11,000 square feet.** Upper floors being converted to living quarters — they're moving in above the shop.
- **Main Street Anniston board member** — not just a beneficiary of the revitalization, he's helping lead it
- Previously part of a **revitalization effort in Decatur** before moving to Anniston
- **Pandemic origin scene:** after work, alone, walking downtown Anniston, noticing the architecture. That's where the idea locked in.
- *"there was no other area in the state that had the beauty in architecture of the buildings like Anniston did"*
- *"got engaged and moved to Calhoun County and bought the building and here we are"* — engagement + migration + purchase as one chain
- **Grand staircase:** 15 oak steps, decorative newel posts, hidden for decades — from Holly's Department Store era. He considers it a hidden treasure.
- Building formerly known as **Griffin's Jewelers**
- Gap he's filling: the **Rabbit Hutch** — a historical downtown gift shop now closed
- **Grand opening: December 5, 2024.** Pre-opening Star article Sept 24, 2024; ribbon cutting article Dec 11, 2024.
- His own words on the mission: *"there was nothing with Anniston in the area, so I wanted to bring that to the city."*

## Profile Angle (Working)

The building on Noble Street has been hiding its own face for seventy years — aluminum facade plastered over 1895 brick. The name nods to something the city already lost (the Old Noble theater). And the man who opened it left Huntsville to do something in a place people are leaving.

The question underneath: what does it mean to open a place, in a city that keeps losing things, and name it after something that's already gone?

Secondary angle: Matt's connection is through the flower farm — a season that ended. Justin kept building. That parallel might matter, but keep Matt in the margins.

## Interview Questions

### Opening — build the room

1. **Before we get to the business — what's the name? Where does "Old Noble" come from?**
   The name is the mystery. Let him answer it. Whatever he says shapes everything else.

2. **Where are you from originally? How did you end up in Huntsville, and then here?**
   Need to know the full geography of his life before Anniston. Decatur revitalization is in the record — let him fill in the rest of the map.

3. **You bought the building with Kevin. How do the two of you divide this — is this his dream too, or is he along for yours?**
   Kevin is co-owner and fiancé. His role in the story is unresolved. Let Justin define it. Could be the most revealing question in the room.

4. **You came from Huntsville. Walk me through the decision to come here instead of somewhere bigger.**
   Forces him to name what Anniston offered that other places didn't — or couldn't.

3. **Walk me through the day you decided this was real. Not the idea — the day you knew you were actually doing it.**
   Specific memory over founding narrative. The doubt and the pivot are more interesting than the plan.

4. **The building you're in has been here since 1895. There's an aluminum facade that's been covering the original brick for seventy years. Did you know that when you moved in? What do you make of it?**
   Connects the physical space to the larger theme. Let him draw the parallel himself if he will.

### Middle — getting at what he built

5. **You've got 5,000 people following a gift shop with no website. That doesn't happen by accident. What are you actually selling them?**
   Not products — the thing underneath. Community, story, permission to buy local, belonging. Let him name it.

6. **What do you stock that you can't explain? The thing where you just know it belongs but you couldn't write a buying policy that would predict it?**
   Gets at the intuition behind curation. What he keeps comes from somewhere specific.

7. **Tell me about a customer you still think about. Not a big purchase — someone who showed up and left something behind.**
   Gift shops are about occasion — birthdays, grief, welcome, apology. One real person in the door opens the whole emotional register.

8. **Anniston has lost things. You opened something. What's the version of this city you're building toward, even if you'd never say it out loud in a pitch to the Chamber of Commerce?**
   Gives him permission to be honest about the place — the harder love, not the booster talk.

### The hard question — only ask when trust is in the room

9. **I want to ask you about something I came across — the City Council meeting last fall, the dispute with Kress Mercantile. I'm not here to try you. But I can't write an honest profile without asking.**
   Don't editorialize. Ask the open question and let silence do its work. Whatever he says — or doesn't — is material.

### Close

10. **What do you know about running this kind of business that you couldn't have learned any other way?**
    Earned wisdom. Bring the conversation back to him on his terms.
