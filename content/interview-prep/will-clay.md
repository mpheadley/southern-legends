> Hey Will — I've been working on a side project called Southern Legends where I profile people around here who are doing interesting work. Attorneys don't usually make the cut, but you're not a usual attorney. Would you want to grab coffee sometime? No agenda beyond a good conversation. I'd love to hear your story.

**Note:** "Attorneys don't usually make the cut" is a compliment disguised as a joke. It sets him apart without flattering him directly and gives him something to be curious about. Don't cut it.

## Profile Angle
Why does a progressive, rugby-playing attorney put his name on a building in a small Alabama city instead of heading somewhere bigger? The question underneath isn't "what does a small-town lawyer do." It's closer to: what kind of person chooses a place that didn't choose him back, and keeps showing up?

## Interview Questions

1. **You could practice law anywhere. Your name is on a building in Anniston, Alabama. What's the version of that story you tell yourself at 2 AM vs. the version you tell at a dinner party?**
   Gets past the polished answer. Everyone has two versions.

2. **Take me back to a specific moment where you thought about leaving. Where were you? What was happening?**
   Scene-level. Forces him into a memory, not a thesis about staying.

3. **You're politically progressive in a place where that's not the path of least resistance. What does that actually cost you day to day? Not the big fights. The small ones.**
   Specificity over abstraction. The small costs are more revealing than the headline ones.

4. **Tell me about a case where the person sitting across from you changed how you think about this place.**
   Lawyers see people at their worst and most honest. Gets at what the work teaches him about the community.

5. **Rugby is a sport where you hit a friend and then have a beer with him. The law is a profession where you argue with someone and then see them at the grocery store. Is that the same skill?**
   Unexpected connection. Links two parts of his life he probably hasn't been asked to connect before.

6. **What's something true about Anniston that people who left don't understand? And what's something true about it that people who stayed don't want to say out loud?**
   Two-sided. Gives him room to love the place and be honest about it. That tension is the whole profile.

7. **Your name is on the building. What did that feel like the first morning you walked in after it went up?**
   Concrete sensory moment. Not "what does it mean to you" but "what did it feel like." Puts him in the scene.
