I've been silent for six months. No facebook posts. No calls or texts. Just withdrew. [Disappeared](/journal/im-not-going-to-disappear). And missed people the whole time. I wanted to reconnect. I had a community, a network. I didn't want to lose that, but I felt like I had.

I walk up and down Littlejohn Road in Pleasant Valley a few times a day. I walk past farms and gardens and fences and houses. Things people have that I had, that I built, or that I was planning to build. There's still hard feelings about that. Heather and I talk about it when we walk.

I still hesitate before looking at the farms and gardens. I look at them anyway.

<FeaturedImage src="/images/journal/little-john-road-dusk.webp" alt="Children playing in the yard at dusk, viewed through a window" caption="Little John Road at dusk" />

---

[Southern Legends](/) was conceived as a "top of the funnel" strategy for [Headley Web](https://headleyweb.com). Giving free value for business owners, hoping the goodwill would help me find clients.

But something was blocking me. Writing about business owners that were succeeding where I had failed. The comparison and jealousy were too hard. So I pocketed the idea. Do cold calling instead.

No baggage with people who didn't know me. Easy.

Getting hung up on sucks.

After basking in rejection, I started asking for referrals.  
Texted 50 friends, “hey its matt, hope you’re doing well! I’m stable now and doing web design. You know anybody?”

It was hard and scary. But it pulled me out. 

I started wondering if building a business could be good for my mental/emotional health.

---

After the texts, I finally started to feel ready to write. I reached out to [Samuel](/profiles/aquality-farms) and [Jean](/profiles/mom-to-go). After writing and sharing them, people read and commented, and that felt really good. I needed some wins. This was a way to win before I had any clients. It felt like I was connecting again. I had something to talk about.

I don't have a pulpit anymore. But I still need a place to process publicly. The pulpit used to be that, but it was limited. Now I write freely. 

---

I keep wondering, though: does an honest, messy journal belong here? On a site I built to profile local business owners? Will it damage my credibility for getting business?

Why do I need to publish this? Isn't a private journal good enough?

I posted anyway.

---

Some clients could be turned off by my history. Bipolar. Hospitalization. Business failure. Maybe those aren't the best clients anyway.

Maybe being broken is what makes the profiles work.

Both the journal and the profiles, on the same domain. Opposing things, held together.
