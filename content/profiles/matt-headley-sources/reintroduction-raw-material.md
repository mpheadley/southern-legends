## Summary of Where This Came From

Tonight's conversation started as a platform strategy question — where should the writing live, how should it be monetized, what do the 27 Patreon subscribers and the CaringBridge/GoFundMe audiences get? It moved through platform comparisons (Substack, Ghost, Patreon, custom build) and landed somewhere unexpected: the question of what Southern Legends actually is.

The decisions made along the way — SL as the owned platform and source of truth, a personal writing section on the site, no Patreon going forward — all came from sitting with a harder question underneath: what do you owe the people who showed up, and what is this site actually for?

The reintroduction post was the thread running through all of it. The structure became clear by the end:

1. The silence — what happened, where you've been
2. Called Coffee — sitting in the car, walking in anyway
3. Southern Legends and what you're beginning to suspect it is
4. An honest non-conclusion: *"I'm not sure yet what to call this. That may become clearer with time."*

The last line is the ending. It does what the gold star does — stops without resolving. Tells the truth about where you actually are.
