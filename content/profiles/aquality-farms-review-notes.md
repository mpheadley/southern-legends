## Voice Guide Review (2026-03-30)

### Kill List Violations
- **Em dash** in opening: "vendors — Heather and I selling flowers" — guide says replace with period, comma, colon, or rewrite. Matt's line, his call.

### Brochure Voice
- "Sam knows the science and he'll tell you about it, because what they're growing has real wellness applications that are only getting more attention." — This is a pitch, not a scene. What does it look like when Sam talks mushroom science?
- "that says something about where agriculture is heading" — vague, could appear in any article about any farm.
- "Go see it. Buy some mushroom extract. Ask Sam about Lion's Mane." — reads like a storefront ad. The guide's test: selling or showing?

### Scene Over Summary (biggest issue)
- Zero scenes in the article. All summary and facts. No room, no sound, no smell, no dialogue.
- What does it look like inside the warehouse? The lights, the humidity, the vertical towers. Is there a hum?
- Has Matt been inside? If so, what does he remember? One visit gives this draft everything it's missing.
- The closing needs a scene, not a CTA. The guide says endings should be "an image, a scene, a question, a line of dialogue — something that opens outward instead of closing down."

### Paragraph Uniformity
- Warehouse and Mission sections stack paragraphs at roughly the same height and rhythm. Every paragraph is 3-4 sentences, same cadence. Guide flags this: "A page where every paragraph is the same height was not written by a person who was thinking."

### Delay the Thesis
- "one of the most interesting things happening in downtown Anniston right now" announces the point immediately. Guide says let meaning accumulate through scene and detail.

### The Subject Is Never Just the Subject
- What's this profile actually about beyond hydroponics? Two brothers inheriting a family business and reinventing it? Feeding a community from a building that always fed it, just differently? The article doesn't have a deeper thread yet.

### Pull Quotes
- Both pull quotes restate facts from the surrounding text. Neither has voice or surprise. Should be the lines a reader remembers.

### What's Working
- Opening is real. Specific details, no performance. Earns the first person.
- "turned a building that used to move other people's produce into a farm that grows its own" — good line, has compression and surprise.
- The Produce Company to Aquality Farms arc is strong structurally.

### The Big Question
Most issues resolve with one thing: **visit the warehouse.** One trip inside gives Matt the scenes, dialogue, sensory details, and ending this draft needs.
