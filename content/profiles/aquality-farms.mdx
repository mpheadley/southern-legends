I met Sam Sawyer at the downtown Anniston farmers market. We were both vendors. Heather and I sold flowers, Sam sold produce. He became a business partner when we started providing flowers for his Harvest Box program. He'd come by the farm weekly to pick up flowers for the box, and he shared mushroom compost with us. We both partnered with Called Coffee to sell our stuff in Jerod's coffee shop. The flower farm closed, but we keep up with Sam's business posts and still bump into him around town.

But the reason I'm writing about Aquality Farms isn't that connection. It's that what he and his brother John Mark are doing in a warehouse on Wilmer Avenue is one of the most interesting things happening in downtown Anniston right now.

The Sawyer brothers are growing 24,000 heads of lettuce and 300 pounds of gourmet mushrooms a week. In downtown Anniston. In a 6,000-square-foot converted warehouse.

Both Auburn graduates. Sam studied horticulture. John Mark grew up in the same family business. Between the two of them, they turned a building that used to move other people's produce into a farm that grows its own.

## The Warehouse

The building at 1414 Wilmer Avenue has been in the Sawyer family for fifty years. Three generations in Anniston. Their grandparents ran Sawyer Anniston Pharmacy from the late sixties until 1999. Their father Mark ran a produce business out of the same building. When Sam and John Mark decided to bring the space back to life, they kept the mission and changed the method. They spent six years studying hydroponics before they touched a wall. At the start of 2024, they began renovating. By April 2025, they cut the ribbon on the storefront.

The front 2,000 square feet is devoted to lettuce and microgreens. Vertical towers grow heads of lettuce in six-week cycles. No soil, no pesticides. Just water, nutrients, and light. 24,000 heads per cycle.

<ArticleImage src="/images/profiles/aquality-farms/lettuce-under-lights.webp" alt="Lettuce growing under colored LED grow lights at Aquality Farms" caption="Lettuce under the grow lights. No soil, no sunlight, no pesticides." layout="center" width={1024} height={768} />

The back 1,000 square feet is the mushroom lab. Lion's Mane, Oyster, Chestnut, Reishi. They started 2026 by building a new fruiting chamber: coated floors, FRP walls, blue-spectrum lights that trigger pinning earlier. A new incubation room is going in now. Sam knows the science behind every species in there, and he'll talk about it if you let him.

<ArticleImage src="/images/profiles/aquality-farms/mushroom-grow-kits.webp" alt="Aquality Farms mushroom grow kits: Blue Oyster, Italian Oyster, and Black Pearl" caption="Blue Oyster, Italian Oyster, Black Pearl. Boxed and ready to grow at home." layout="left" width={1200} height={900} />

They're also expanding outside. A fence is going up on the south side of the property for outdoor growing systems. More lettuce, more capacity, less warehouse ceiling.

## The Mission

They're partnered with the Alabama Farm to School Program, supplying pesticide-free produce to over 35,000 students. Kids in the region eating food that was grown downtown, without chemicals, by two brothers who decided their family's old warehouse should mean something.

<ArticleImage src="/images/profiles/aquality-farms/products-spread.webp" alt="Aquality Farms products: butter lettuce, microgreens, oyster mushrooms, and mushroom tincture bottles" caption="What comes out of the warehouse: lettuce, microgreens, oyster mushrooms, and their own line of tinctures." layout="right" width={2048} height={1536} />

The storefront sells direct to the public. Fresh produce, herbs, mushroom tinctures, ultrasonic mushroom extracts, microgreen powders. They also put together a "Harvest Box" with other local farms and businesses.

<ArticleImage src="/images/profiles/aquality-farms/harvest-box.webp" alt="Aquality Farms Harvest Box with Golden Oyster mushrooms, butter lettuce, microgreens, sweet potatoes, and other local farm products" caption="The Harvest Box: Golden Oyster mushrooms, lettuce, microgreens, and produce from other local farms. One box, multiple growers." layout="left" width={1536} height={2048} />

This year they launched a School Sponsorship Program, letting local businesses and organizations fund pesticide-free produce for students directly. Growing the food was the first problem. Paying for it to reach kids was the second. The sponsorship program solves the second one.

Aquality Farms is a Sweet Grown Alabama member. No acreage. No land at all. Just a warehouse and a method.

## Chaha Outdoors

Sam also runs Chaha Outdoors. The name comes from the Creek word for "high place," the same root as Cheaha Mountain, thirty minutes from Anniston.

<ArticleImage src="/images/profiles/aquality-farms/sam-chaha-trail.webp" alt="Sam Sawyer on a trail run in Calhoun County" caption="Same guy, different setting. Sam on the trails through Chaha Outdoors." layout="right" width={960} height={720} />

Chaha is Sam's outdoor wellness arm. He leads wellness walks at community events, shows up at races with the Anniston Runners Club, and advocates for the trails that run through Calhoun County: Chief Ladiga, Coldwater Mountain, Pinhoti. He attended the Yellowhammer Outdoor Development symposium this year. He's a charter member of NAWA, the Northeast Alabama Wellness Alliance.

The connection to the farm is the same philosophy from a different angle. The farm grows food that's good for you. Chaha gets you outside, which is also good for you. One feeds the body from a warehouse. The other does it from a trailhead.

## Why This Matters for Anniston

Downtown Anniston has been through cycles of decline and revival for decades. Every new business that opens downtown is a bet that the revitalization is real. Sam and John Mark made that bet with a warehouse, some vertical towers, and a mushroom lab. And they're winning. Not in the flashy sense, but in the pounds-of-produce-per-week sense. The kind of winning that feeds kids.

The storefront is open Monday through Friday, 9 to 5:30, and Saturdays 10 to 4. Walk in and ask Sam about Lion's Mane. He'll tell you more than you expected.
