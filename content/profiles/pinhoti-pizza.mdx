I wrote about the Chief Ladiga Trail earlier on this site — the 40-mile paved path from Anniston to the Georgia state line, the Silver Comet connection, the $9.7 million in economic impact. I mentioned Piedmont as the trail town at the heart of it all. What I should have led with is the pizza.

Pinhoti Pizza Company sits at 107 South Main Street in Piedmont, exactly where the Chief Ladiga Trail crosses the downtown grid. The patio is on the trail. You can ride your bike to the door, lean it against the railing, and be eating brick oven pizza ten minutes later. If that sounds like it was designed specifically for cyclists and hikers, that's because it was — and it's working.

## Maggie and Kevin Cunningham

The Cunninghams opened Pinhoti Pizza in November 2024. In less than a year and a half, it already feels like it's been part of Piedmont forever — which is the highest compliment you can pay a restaurant in a small town. They named it after the Pinhoti Trail, the long-distance hiking trail that crosses the nearby mountains and connects to the Appalachian Trail in Georgia. The name is a declaration of intent: this restaurant belongs to the trails.

They make their own dough from unbleached flour. They churn their own marinara from whole tomatoes — no preservatives, no citric acid. Everything goes into a six-foot Woodstone brick oven, and what comes out is the kind of pizza that makes people drive to Piedmont on purpose. The "Pied-Monster" is their signature — a loaded, meaty thing that justifies the name. But even a margherita from that oven is worth the trip.

## The Space

Walk inside and the first thing you notice is that the walls are decorated with trail maps, kayaks, bikes, and backpacking equipment. It's not just decor — it's a conversation starter. The Cunninghams want you thinking about what's outside while you eat, and then they want you to go do it. Piedmont sits at the convergence of the Chief Ladiga Trail, the Pinhoti Trail, and Terrapin Creek. Bike, hike, paddle — all of it starts from the same downtown grid where the pizza is.

Pinhoti Pizza is the first restaurant in Piedmont located directly on the Chief Ladiga Trail. That distinction matters more than it sounds. For years, the trail brought cyclists through Piedmont, but the infrastructure to serve them — food, coffee, gear — was catching up slowly. Pinhoti Pizza filled the most important gap. Nobody rides 20 miles and wants a gas station sandwich. They want real food, made well, in a place that understands why they're there.

Backpackers use Piedmont and Pinhoti Pizza as a resupply and celebration stop on the Pinhoti Trail. Cyclists make it a turnaround point or a midway refuel. Locals come because the pizza is genuinely good and the price is right. The business model works because the trails deliver the customers and the kitchen delivers the food.

## What It Means for Piedmont

I've written about what the trail economy means for Calhoun County — the JSU economic impact study, the $9.7 million figure, the businesses clustering around access points. Pinhoti Pizza is the most visible example of that economy working at street level. A couple saw an opportunity at the intersection of a trail and a Main Street, bet on brick oven pizza, and created something that anchors the entire downtown.

<PullQuote>A couple saw an opportunity at the intersection of a trail and a Main Street, and created something that anchors the entire downtown.</PullQuote>

Elevated Grounds is steps away serving coffee. Ladiga Trail Trikes is nearby. Main Street South has vendors. The Eubanks Welcome Center greets visitors. But Pinhoti Pizza is the draw — the place that turns a rest stop into a destination and gives people a reason to tell their friends about Piedmont.

The Cunninghams are open Thursday through Sunday. If you're riding the Chief Ladiga, plan your ride so you hit Piedmont at lunchtime. If you're driving, it's worth the trip from anywhere in Calhoun County. Order the Pied-Monster. Trust me.
