## Sacred Heart, Pruning Shears

Lewis went to Sacred Heart Catholic School. I was a member of Sacred Heart Catholic Church during high school. He pointed that out in the store — he knew a lot of the same people I knew from there. It landed the way those things do in small cities.

On a later visit he sold me a pair of pruning shears. He mentioned they were his favorites — he used to work for a landscaping company in high school, and those were the shears he used then. He still knew which ones held an edge.

Lewis was pretty talkative. RD was the same way — warm, encouraging. Between the two of them, you got the full picture of a place where somebody knew what they were selling and actually cared who they were selling it to.


## The Alley Market

RD was very encouraging and complimentary about what we were doing with the farm. He would come to the farmer's market when we were selling flowers. One year — I think the second year we were vendors — the market was actually out behind RD's in his alleyway. He hosted it on his property. They would both come and buy flowers, and Lewis would buy eggs. They always chatted with Heather and me. We had a great relationship with them as patrons. They patronized us. We patronized them.
