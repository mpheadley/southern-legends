## How to use this doc

For each stream:
1. **What it is** — one sentence.
2. **Why it fits (or doesn't).** Does it serve the editorial mission or cannibalize it?
3. **Audience.** Who pays. Where they come from. How many it takes to matter.
4. **Price / unit economics.** Rough numbers. Don't over-engineer.
5. **Effort to launch.** Hours / weeks / months.
6. **Dependencies.** What has to exist first (published volume, audience size, skill, vendor).
7. **Risk / downside.** Credibility, time drain, platform lock-in, tax/legal.
8. **Decision gate.** The specific condition under which this moves from scaffold → committed.

Don't fill every field now. Fill what's honest. Leave the rest blank.

## Stream 5 — Merchandise / Apparel

- **What it is:**
- **Product ideas to size:** T-shirts, hats, tote bags, bumper stickers, enamel pins, notecards/stationery with SL illustrations, print-at-home wallpaper with the topo pattern.
- **Editorial fit test:** A shirt that quotes a line from a profile can work. A generic logo tee probably doesn't. Which side of the line is each idea on?
- **Designs:** (topo pattern already exists — `topo-pattern.svg`; Parchment & Pine palette is strong for apparel. List 2–3 specific shirt concepts before committing.)
- **Vendor:**
  - **Print-on-demand** (Printful, Printify, Cotton Bureau): zero inventory risk, low margin (~$5–10/shirt), slower shipping. Right choice for MVP.
  - **Small-batch local print** (a NE Alabama screen printer): higher margin, inventory risk, tighter color control, story value ("printed in Anniston").
- **Audience size needed:** Break-even on a 24-shirt minimum run is ~15 sales at $30. Is that realistic in the next 12 months? Be honest.
- **Storefront:** Shopify is overkill. Stripe Payment Links + a simple `/shop` page on SL probably enough to start. Revisit if volume justifies it.
- **Effort to launch:** 1–2 weekends for POD MVP. 3–4 weeks for a local-print run.
- **Risk / downside:** Inventory sitting in the garage. Apparel that feels off-brand. Fulfillment stealing writing time.
- **Decision gate:** First product ships when _____ (e.g., "one specific line from a profile is worth putting on a shirt and we have 100+ email subscribers").

## Stream 7 — Sponsorship / Underwriting

- **What it is:** Local business sponsors a profile series or a newsletter issue. Not ad placements — NPR-style underwriting copy.
- **Why it might fit:** SL is already the warm-lead layer for local businesses. Some of them would pay to be named as supporters.
- **Why it might not:** Risks editorial independence. A sponsor today is a conflict-of-interest tomorrow when you want to profile their competitor or critique them.
- **Rule to write down:** No sponsor gets profile coverage during their sponsorship window, period. If that rule feels too tight to survive, kill the stream.
- **Decision gate:** Only pursued if _____.

## Stream 8 — Teaching / Workshops

- **What it is:** Paid workshops on longform profile writing, small-town storytelling, or "building an editorial site as a marketing funnel" (the actual Headley Web sales pitch in educational form).
- **Formats:** 1-hour Zoom, half-day in-person, multi-week cohort, async video course.
- **Audience:** Other small-town writers, small-business owners who want to write about their work, ministry folks pivoting to writing.
- **Risk:** Course-building is a known time sink. Don't start one unless the demand is already visible (people are asking unprompted).
- **Decision gate:** First workshop when _____ (e.g., "5 unsolicited asks for it").

## Stream 9 — Licensing / Syndication (inbound)

- **What it is:** Outlets reprint SL profiles for a fee.
- **Current reality (per PUBLISHING-PLAN):** Matt is the one pitching *out* — Anniston Star, Calhoun Journal. Right now syndication is a distribution play, not a revenue play.
- **When it flips:** If a regional or national outlet (Garden & Gun, Bitter Southerner, The Local Palate) asks to reprint with payment. Don't build infrastructure for this until it happens.
- **Decision gate:** First inbound ask.

## Streams to explicitly NOT pursue (write these down so they stop coming back)

- **YouTube channel** — video production is a different job. Skip.
- **Podcast as primary stream** — a single episode or two fits inside the journal. A weekly show does not.
- **Paid Discord / community platform** — not the audience, not the posture.
- **NFTs / crypto drops** — no.
- **Affiliate / Amazon links** — erodes editorial trust for pennies.

Add to this list as temptations come up. Named and killed is better than lingering.
